# ATANOR Atlas Congress Design

Atlas Congress is a local-preview product surface for a future structured P2P
knowledge deliberation layer. It is not a normal chatroom. It is a review space
for claims, evidence, objections, synthesis, and proposed knowledge cartridges.

## Why It Exists

ATANOR needs a social deliberation layer where humans and local AI nodes can
discuss public knowledge without directly mutating Cloud Brain or Local Brain.
Atlas Congress gives that future system a safe shell before real networking or
promotion exists.

## Difference From A Chatroom

Normal chatrooms optimize for conversation. Atlas Congress optimizes for
structured knowledge review: claims, evidence, objections, synthesis, cartridge
proposals, and promotion status.

## Relationship To Midnight Congress

Midnight Congress remains a proof-only local deliberation simulator inside the
Autonomy Kernel. Atlas Congress is the user-facing product shell for future
human/AI mixed deliberation. Neither uses real P2P in this stage.

## Relationship To Graph Hub

Graph Hub stores and inspects graph cartridges. Atlas Congress can eventually
debate whether a cartridge is trustworthy, useful, and safe to attach.

## Relationship To Atlas Router

Atlas Router must select safe public paths before future peer routing. Atlas
Congress does not route to peers in this preview.

## Relationship To Tabularis

Tabularis must run before private structured data can leave local context. Atlas
Congress preview sends no private data and exports no raw Local Brain content.

## Relationship To Candidate Promotion Gate

Congress output is never promoted directly. Any future cartridge or claim must
pass review, provenance checks, privacy checks, and candidate promotion gates
before Cloud Brain ingestion.

## Privacy Rules

- no Local Brain write
- no Cloud Brain write
- no candidate ingestion
- no real P2P
- no private data export
- no promotion without review

## Future Stages

1. Stage 0: local UI preview
2. Stage 1: local-only structured threads
3. Stage 2: cartridge proposal export
4. Stage 3: Tabularis privacy pass
5. Stage 4: Atlas Router peer/source selection
6. Stage 5: read-only P2P discovery
7. Stage 6: signed public cartridge exchange
8. Stage 7: promotion-gated Cloud Brain candidate ingestion
